--- layout: archive author_profile: true --- Hello world I study minds and brains to create AI. I was a Senior Research Scientist at DeepMind in the Deep Learning team between 2016 and 2019. Before that I was a PhD student at MIT in Josh Tenenbaum's group in the Brain & Cognitive Sciences and CSAIL departments. My research has led to the development of primarily three areas: (1) vision as neural inverse graphics, (2) hierarchical and structured deep reinforcement learning, and (3) probabilistic programming.

Research Summary:

  • What kind of programs can capture visual and motor complexity?

    Percetion in our brains runs in both conditional (wake) and unconditional (sleep) modes. How do generative perceptual programs evolve and adapt during our life time? What are the inductive biases or simplest set of initial program spaces? Can graphics and game engines provide a general prior for visual perception? SPIRAL is our latest attempt at using Deep Reinforcement Learning agents for inverse graphics. See my demo of SPIRAL running on a robot. Picture (Best Paper Honorable Mention Award at CVPR 2015) was my PhD work to learn inverse graphics probabilistic programs conditioned on images and accelerated/enabled by deep learning. DC-IGN is a convolutional net and a learning algorithm to learn a neural rendering engine given raw videos.

  • What bottom-up sensory abstractions do we need for efficient, explorative and curious behavior?

    Hierarchical DQN (hDQN) is a hierarchical deep RL agent that explores in the space of entities and geometric/logical relationships between them. Deep Successor Representations is an architecture to estimate goal-driven model-free representations for control.

  • Can games help agents to learn and ground language?

    I spent many hours in my childhood playing text based multi-user dungeons. This is how I primarily learnt about English. This motivated us to prototype Deep RL agents to learn to play text-based games and write a paper about it (EMNLP 2015 Best Paper Honorable Mention Award).
